Importance of detecting Outliers/unusuall values

Outliers can significantly impact the results of statistical analyses, leading to inaccurate conclusions.
Outliers can skew the distribution of data, affecting the mean and standard deviation calculations.
This can lead to misleading interpretations of the data. By knowing the Data Distribution,
we can adjust our expectations related to ML-Models and take appropriate measures to handle the problem.

Varity in the datast

variety in a dataset is essential for conducting thorough analyses, making informed decisions, improving predictive modeling accuracy, ensuring data quality.
In the case of a housing dataset where 75% of the houses have 2 or 3 bedrooms, the resulting predictive model may perform well in predicting prices for houses with 2 or 3 bedrooms due to the abundance of data in that category. However, if the dataset lacks variety and does not include sufficient representation of other types of houses (e.g., houses with 1 bedroom, 4+ bedrooms), the model may not generalize well to predict prices for these less represented categories.
This lack of varietycan lead to biases and inaccuracies in the predictions for those underrepresented categories.

Power graphical presentation of data

In the case of a housing dataset, using histograms and box plots can help us quickly identify outliers in the data. Histograms provide a visual representation of the distribution of a numerical variable, allowing us to see any unusual patterns or extreme values. Box plots, on the other hand, show the spread and central tendency of the data, making it easy to spot outliers based on their position outside the whiskers.
